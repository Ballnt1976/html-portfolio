The Best Movies According to Neil Thomas Ball

My Top 3 movies of all time.

Lord of the Rings - the Fellowship of the Rings

Peter Jackson’s The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring is a breathtaking adaptation of J.R.R. Tolkien’s epic fantasy. The film masterfully introduces Middle-earth, bringing its landscapes, lore, and characters to life with stunning visuals and a sweeping score. From the peaceful Shire to the shadowy depths of Moria, the journey is both visually and emotionally captivating. Elijah Wood shines as Frodo Baggins, while Ian McKellen’s Gandalf adds wisdom and gravitas, making the ensemble cast truly unforgettable.

The film balances action, adventure, and heartfelt moments, setting the stage for an epic trilogy. The battle sequences, especially the climactic fight at Amon Hen, are intense and beautifully choreographed. Howard Shore’s musical score enhances the film’s grandeur, making every scene feel larger than life. The Fellowship of the Ring is a cinematic triumph that immerses audiences in a world of bravery, friendship, and destiny, leaving them eager for the next chapter.

Avengers End Game

Marvel Studios' Avengers: Endgame is a thrilling and emotional conclusion to the Infinity Saga, delivering an unforgettable experience for fans. The film masterfully balances action, humor, and heartfelt moments as Earth's Mightiest Heroes embark on a desperate mission to undo Thanos’ devastating snap. With stunning visual effects, epic battle sequences, and deeply personal character arcs, the movie offers both spectacle and emotional weight, making it a standout in the superhero genre.

The final act, featuring the legendary battle against Thanos, is a cinematic triumph filled with jaw-dropping moments and powerful callbacks to the MCU’s history. Robert Downey Jr.’s performance as Tony Stark is particularly moving, providing a fitting and heroic end to his journey. Avengers: Endgame is not just a superhero film—it’s a love letter to fans, a celebration of 11 years of storytelling, and a deeply satisfying conclusion that cements its place as one of the greatest blockbuster films of all time.

Indiana Jones and the Raiders of the Lost Ark

Steven Spielberg’s Raiders of the Lost Ark is a non-stop adventure that redefined the action genre. Harrison Ford delivers an iconic performance as Indiana Jones, a charismatic archaeologist who battles Nazis while searching for the mystical Ark of the Covenant. The film blends thrilling action, humor, and mystery with breathtaking set pieces, from the rolling boulder escape to the intense truck chase.

What makes Raiders so memorable is its perfect mix of practical effects, clever storytelling, and John Williams’ unforgettable score. The chemistry between Indiana and Marion (Karen Allen) adds heart to the adventure, while the villains are menacing without overshadowing the hero. Every scene feels fresh and exciting, keeping audiences on the edge of their seats.

More than 40 years later, Raiders of the Lost Ark remains a gold standard for action-adventure films. Its blend of classic serial influences, witty dialogue, and expertly choreographed sequences make it as entertaining today as it was in 1981. Indiana Jones isn’t just a character—he’s a legend, and this film is where his unforgettable journey begins.
